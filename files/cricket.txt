Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players.
The game is played on a field at the centre of which is a 22-yard pitch with a wicket at each end.
One team bats, trying to score as many runs as possible, while the other team bowls and fields.
Runs are scored by hitting the ball with the bat and running between the wickets.
The teams switch between batting and fielding at the end of an innings.
Cricket has three main formats: Test cricket, One Day International (ODI), and Twenty20 (T20).
The International Cricket Council (ICC) is the governing body of the sport.
The Cricket World Cup is the most prestigious tournament in ODI cricket.
Legendary players include Sir Don Bradman, Sachin Tendulkar, and Shane Warne.
Cricket is particularly popular in England, Australia, India, Pakistan, and the West Indies.
